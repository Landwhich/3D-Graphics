Assignment 03 - Image Based Lighting

Summary of Task

The goal of Assignment 3 was to capture a real-world environment using a chrome mirror ball or fisheye lens, process the photographs into a HDR image, unwrap it into a panorama, and use the result as Image Based Lighting in Autodesk Maya with the Arnold renderer. A 3D model was to then be placed into the scene and rendered with the IBL derived from the scene.

Method / Approach

The workflow followed the tutorial pipeline: HDR capture of a chrome mirror ball, merging in Photoshop, panorama unwrapping in Hugin, and final IBL rendering in Maya.

HDR Capture and Merge

Multiple exposures of a chrome mirror ball were taken to capture the full dynamic range of the scene. These were merged into a single 32-bit HDR image using Photoshop's Merge to HDR tool and exported as an OpenEXR file. The dark look of the resulting image was expected, as the room itself was low-light, this is handled correctly by the HDR format.

Hugin Panorama Unwrapping

The EXR was loaded into Hugin Panorama Stitcher to unwrap the mirror ball projection into a panorama suitable for IBL. Because Hugin does not include a Mirror Ball lens type by default in the available version, the Orthographic lens type was used as the closest equivalent to mirror ball projection. Control points were manually added, to compensate for this. After re-aligning with the corrected lens type and fiddling with some optimizations as well as yaw, pitch, and roll, the panorama was stitched and exported.

IBL Setup in Maya

The starter Maya scene was opened and the panorama was connected to an Arnold aiSkyDomeLight node as the IBL source. A 9V battery model (Not my model) was created and placed into the scene. The aiShadowMatte material was used on the desk surface to catch realistic contact shadows from the model. Render settings were configured in the Arnold Renderer to export an exr file for its transparency property. The light of my room's desk lamp and monitor was captured naturally by the IBL.

Challenges and Mistakes

Several difficulties were encountered during the pipeline:

  1. I had configured my camera to capture in JPEG format, which I had only discovered after having returned the mirror ball.
  2. A broken tripod was also unhelpful and resulted in some video shaky-ness, as well as some ghostliness in the HDR photo.
  3. Working with the mirror ball presented inherent constraints: the camera must remain in a fixed position pointing at the ball, which limits angular variety and makes control point matching more difficult. The absence of a native Mirror Ball lens type in the installed version of Hugin added further complexity.

Additional Information

In retrospect, a fisheye lens approach would have been preferable to the mirror ball. A fisheye shoot allows multiple angled captures from different orientations, producing more control points and a more complete panorama with better panorama alignment.

References

Maya documentation and course lecture notes (T07 – Image Based Lighting) were used as primary references for the IBL setup, Arnold renderer configuration, and Maya workflow. Hugin's official documentation was referenced for control point placement and lens type selection.
